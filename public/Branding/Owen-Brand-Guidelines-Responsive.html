BDo & Don't

Do

The phrasing we want associated with Owen.

  • "We can be there this afternoon."Confirms speed without overselling.
  • "Here's what it'll cost — before we start."Plain pricing, plain promise.
  • "Family-owned since 1976."Heritage stated once, no embellishment.
  • "Licensed, insured, and on time."Three things that matter, in seven words.

Don't

Phrasing that pulls Owen toward generic-contractor territory.

  • "Your one-stop solution for all plumbing needs!"Empty, salesy, says nothing.
  • "We dispatch certified hydraulic technicians…"Jargon. No homeowner says "hydraulic."
  • "Don't let leaks ruin your day!"Fear-based, exclamatory, off-brand.
  • "Award-winning service excellence."Vague superlatives erode trust.

Do

What Owen photography looks like.

  • Use natural light — soft window light wins every time.
  • Photograph real Owen people, in real homes.
  • Show the craft — pipework, hands, fittings, tools.
  • Keep color treatment quiet: subtle warmth, gentle contrast.

Don't

Imagery that pulls us into generic-contractor territory.

  • Don't use staged-handshake or thumbs-up stock photos.
  • Don't use HDR, heavy filters, or saturated grading.
  • Don't show distress imagery — flooded floors, fear scenes.
  • Don't use the cartoon plumber illustration. Retire it.
